About the Visualization

This interface visualizes a thermodynamic extension of quantum mechanics, where energy (\( E \)), entropy change (\( \Delta S \)), and residual entropy (\( S_r \)) drive the evolution of quantum states (\( \psi' \)) under the Second Law of Thermodynamics. Particles transition from disorder to structured patterns, reflecting the emergence of information and complexity. Adjust the sliders to explore how these parameters shape the system, and reset the animation to restart.

Parameters:

  • Energy (\( E \)): Drives particle motion and pattern formation.
  • Entropy Change (\( \Delta S \)): Controls the transformation of disorder into structured information.
  • Residual Entropy (\( S_r \)): Adds randomness, representing unprocessed disorder.
